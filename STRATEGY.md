# Strategy 101 for price management in real estate

After listing the property, you will receive digital and physical interest signals helping you adjust the price. Your strategy is to adjust the price, before the property becomes stale (30 days).

The signals map into a conversion funnel:

### 1. Digital Engagement Signals (The Top of the Funnel)
These are the earliest indicators of whether your price is aligned with the digital "sniff test."
* **Impression-to-Click Ratio:** If your home appears in searches but isn't being clicked, your lead photo or price is likely the deterrent. In 2026, a CTR (Click-Through Rate) below **1.5%** in most urban hubs is a signal to drop the price or change the thumbnail.
* **Save-to-View Ratio:** High views with low "Saves" suggest your home is a "looky-loo" property. High "Saves" with zero showing requests suggest the property is a "maybe"–likely priced just **3–5%** above the buyer's actual strike zone.
* **The 48-Hour Momentum Spike:** New listings typically see a massive spike in the first two days. If your views on Day 3 are less than **20%** of your Day 1 views, your initial price did not capture the "buyer-in-waiting" pool.

### 2. Physical Interest & Showing Signals
Once the property is live, physical traffic provides the "ground truth" for your algorithm.
* **Showing Volume Thresholds:** * **< 2 showings/week:** Severe price/market mismatch. A **5–10%** cut is usually required to restart momentum.
    * **3–6 showings/week:** Healthy interest; the price is likely accurate but may need a small "nudge" or concession to close.
    * **7+ showings/week:** High demand; price is likely at or below market value.
* **Second Showing Rate:** This is a high-conviction signal. If you have 10 showings and 0 "second looks," the price is likely fine for the neighborhood, but the property's condition doesn't justify the premium.
* **Agent Feedback (NLP Data):** If you are scraping agent feedback, look for keywords like "dark," "dated," or "layout." In a price optimization model, these are **condition-adjusted price signals.** A "dated" kitchen typically requires a **$20,000–$40,000** price offset in the 2026 mid-market.

### 3. Market & Competitive Signals
You are not pricing in a vacuum; you are pricing against the "Next Best Alternative."
* **The "Under Contract" Comp:** Monitor nearby homes that were listed *after* yours. If they go "Sale Pending" within 7 days and yours is still active, they have effectively poached your buyer. This is a "Trailing Signal" that your price is the ceiling for the neighborhood.
* **Neighborhood Absorption Rate:** This measures the speed at which homes are selling. 
$$\text{Absorption Rate} = \frac{\text{Sales per Month}}{\text{Active Listings}}$$

If the monthly rate drops below **15%**, you are in a buyer's market, and your algorithm should prioritize aggressive "first-mover" price cuts.

### 4. Macro-Economic Signals 
* **Mortgage Rate Volatility:** Even a **0.25%** weekly swing in the 10-year Treasury yield can immediately freeze buyer pools. If rates spike on a Tuesday, your weekend open house traffic will likely drop by **30%**, which is a macro signal, not a house-specific one.
* **Inventory Influx:** If your local zip code sees a **10%+** increase in new listings in a single week, your "scarcity premium" evaporates, and your price should be adjusted downward to maintain its "Featured" status in search results.

### Signal-to-Action Matrix for Your Algorithm

| Signal Pattern | Likely Diagnosis | Recommended Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| High Views, Low Clicks | Lead Photo or Price | Drop price **3%** or rotate photos |
| High Clicks, Low Saves | Property Condition | Offer "Repair Credit" or staging |
| High Saves, Low Showings | Price Gap | Drop price to next **$10k** psychological bracket |
| Many Showings, No Offers | Competitive Poaching | Check "Recently Pending" comps; match their price |
